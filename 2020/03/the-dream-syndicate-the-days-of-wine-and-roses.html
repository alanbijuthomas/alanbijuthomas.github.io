The Dream Syndicate - The Days of Wine and Roses

Ruby Records, 1982, paisley underground

24th March 2020


The Paisley Underground scene was one of many interesting movements taking place in the early 80s as California bands filtered their love for the 60s through a more modern punk template. While their contemporaries were indebted to psychedelia, The Dream Syndicate worshipped The Velvet Underground. Doing his best Lou Reed impression, Steve Wynn spills out his anxious, existential thoughts over some beautiful guitar interplay. The Days of Wine and Roses strikes a fine balance between feedback-drenched noise and the jittery jangle that would go on to dominate the 80s indie scene. From the wry smile of 'Tell Me When It's Over' to the tension of 'Halloween' to the pounding title track that wraps things up, this album is jam-packed with great moments and it's a shame it's not recognised as a classic record.
