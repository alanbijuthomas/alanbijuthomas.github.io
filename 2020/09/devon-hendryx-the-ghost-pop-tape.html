Devon Hendryx - The Ghost~Pop Tape

(No Label), 2013, ambient pop/cloud rap

27th September 2020


Before all the COVID-19 chaos, I got to see JPEGMAFIA live at Manchester Academy. It was a great show and someone in the crowd asked for his rendition of Carly Rae Jepsen's 'Call Me Maybe', but he didn't have the backing track so instead, we were treated to 'Rainbow Six'. This month, he added the aforementioned cover to streaming and I finally decided to check out the album it's from. The Ghost~Pop Tape is not industrial, glitchy or hardcore like the work he's known for now. Instead, Peggy gives us a depressive mash of ambient pop, cloud rap and vaporwave which can only be described as vibey. There's similarities to Dean Blunt's sampledelia or perhaps even the work of Arthur Russell, at least in terms of atmosphere. I love the dark, lo-fi sound and while it suffers from being a bit too samey, it's definitely worth checking out to see his evolution as an artist.
