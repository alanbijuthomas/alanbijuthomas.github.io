Pink Siifu - NEGRO

Field-Left, 2020, sound collage/industrial

7th May 2020


My first introduction to Pink Siifu was on Slauson Malone's 2019 release. He also appeared on The Avalanches' new song 'Running Red Lights' with Rivers Cuomo, paying a tribute to the late David Berman. Neither feature nor a quick flick through his previous work could have prepared me for his new album. NEGRO is an aggressive affair filled to the brim with righteous anger at the inherently racist institutions that make life a struggle for black people in America. Pink Siifu intricately layers samples and voice clips with sections of harsh noise, hardcore punk and the occasional verse to paint a portrait of racist America. After repeated listens, I've come to appreciate just how effectively and creatively he expresses his fury, mainly at the police and rightfully so. 'FK' is one of the best expressions of rage I've ever heard in a song. Unfortunately, with the current president's likely victory in the upcoming 2020 election, I can't see any of Pink Siifu's anger subsiding.

EDIT: Not even 2 months after its release, the album's message is further justified by the killing of George Floyd at the hands of 4 police officers.
