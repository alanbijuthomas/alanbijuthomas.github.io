Avey Tare and Panda Bear - Spirit They're Gone, Spirit They've Vanished

Animal, 2000, neo-psychedelia/experimental

18th May 2020


Now that everything's locked down, I've been talking long walks in forests to keep myself sane. On my last one, I decided to throw on what's now known as the first Animal Collective album. It's really more of an Avey Tare solo effort with drumming provided by Panda Bear. Spirit They're Gone, Spirit They've Vanished is a deeply moving record that captures the magic of childlike wonder and also comes to terms with its inevitable loss. The album features twinkling, fairy-like melodies juxtaposed with cacophonous, screeching noise, mimicking the highs and lows of growing up. While it's not perfect, songs like 'April And The Phantom', 'Chocolate Girl' and 'Alvin Row' are easily some of the band's best. Childhood is a theme that has been explored throughout Animal Collective's work and this is where it all began, with them accepting their loss of innocence and realising it's okay.
