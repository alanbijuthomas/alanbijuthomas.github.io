Pink Floyd - The Piper at the Gates of Dawn

Columbia (EMI), 1967, psychedelic rock/psychedelic pop

20th December 2020


I've never really fallen in love with Pink Floyd's 70s masterpieces but The Piper at the Gates of Dawn has always been one of my favourite 60s albums. Similar to Mercury Rev, I prefer the first, more psychedelic era of the band featuring one of rock's greatest visionaries in Syd Barrett. 1967 saw the release of many groundbreaking records and the Pink Floyd debut is certainly up there. The record is dominated by Syd's whimsical fantasies, alternating between melodic pop and daring experimentation in its attempt to capture his vision. The astral space rock that opens either side of the album remains the most gripping: 'Astronomy Domine' is a signal sent into space by the band while the noodling, hisses and echoes of 'Interstellar Overdrive' never fail to shock a first time listener. On the other end of the spectrum, there's even wacky nursery rhymes that you could play to your kids in 'The Gnome' and 'Bike'. The Piper at the Gates of Dawn is Syd's masterpiece and in my opinion, the only essential Pink Floyd record.
