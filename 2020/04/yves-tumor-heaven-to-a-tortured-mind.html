Yves Tumor - Heaven to a Tortured Mind

Warp Records, 2020, neo-psychedelia/art rock

11th April 2020


On their second release for Warp Records, Yves Tumor departs from the industrial, hypnagogic textures of Safe in the Hands of Love. Instead, they combine their experimental tendencies with the funk/soul combos of Prince and the acid rock of Hendrix, developing into a full-blown, genre-hopping rock star. Although Yves and their guests stick to fawning over a doomed love affair, it never gets old as the album flows brilliantly over its 36-minute runtime. Two-part 'Romanticist / Dream Palette' is one of my favourites as psychedelic soul transitions seamlessly into an eruption of shiny synths, perhaps summing up Yves' incredible versatility. However, second single 'Kerosene!' remains my favourite track on the album as they dive head first into psychedelic rock with an explosive guitar solo worthy of Purple Rain. Yves Tumor has certainly surpassed all expectations and I'm sure Heaven to a Tortured Mind will stay in my rotation throughout the year.
