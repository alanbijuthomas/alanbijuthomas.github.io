The Pop Group - Y

Radar Records, 1979, post-punk/experimental rock

3rd July 2020


There was all sorts of wacky stuff going on during the punk explosion of the late 1970s but I'd have to say the award of weirdest band would have to go to The Pop Group. Their debut record features atonal, jagged guitar lines that you could perhaps find on a no wave release but that's the only thing resembling punk on here. The rest of the album's sound is a mix of twisted funk, spacey dub and dissonant free jazz over which Mark Stewart switches his vocal inflection with practically every line. The album's sound and lyrics evoke images of a post-apocalyptic world or in The Pop Group's view, describe the barbaric nature of the capitalist reality we are living in. Y is one of the hardest post-punk albums to wrap your head around, but its ominous, rhythmic brew of 70s sounds and radical politics can also be one of the most rewarding. Just have a listen to 'She Is Beyond Good And Evil'.
