Fela Kuti - Expensive Shit

Sounds Workshop Records, 1975, afrobeat

22nd October 2020


Fela Kuti was one of the most innovative artists ever, fusing highlife, funk and jazz with traditional Yoruba music to create the genre we now call afrobeat. Every album he recorded was great but Expensive Shit remains my favourite, partly due to its fascinating backstory which is explained on the cover. On the title track, playful polyrhythms start and maintain an irresistible groove before Fela hops in with a sax solo. The horn section joins in and the chanting starts, as the band derides the Nigerian government. 'Water No Get Enemy' is one of his most beloved tracks as he riffs on water's importance to life in both Yoruba and English. Fela's hypnotic jams touched the mind, body and soul, inspiring artists like Talking Heads whose afrobeat-indebted classic Remain in Light just turned 40. If you've never listened to Fela Kuti, you're in for a treat. I guarantee you will not regret it.
