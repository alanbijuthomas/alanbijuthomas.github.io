The Red Krayola - The Parable of Arable Land

International Artists, 1967, experimental rock/psychedelic rock

2nd June 2020


The Red Krayola's debut album The Parable of Arable Land is one of my favourite records from the 60s. The band made very psychedelic music in the sense that their songs were vivid, visceral and overwhelming, similar to the experience of a trip. When people talk about this album, they tend to focus on the free form freak-outs that comprise half the record. While I have always appreciated those interludes of pure schizophrenic chaos, I initially fell in love with this record because of its other half. With frenzied playing and surreal lyricism that goes far beyond the usual hippie tropes, The Red Krayola made some of psychedelic rock's best songs in 'Hurricane Fighter Plane', 'Transparent Radiation', 'War Sucks' and 'Pink Stainless Tail'.
