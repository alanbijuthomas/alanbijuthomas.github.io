Life Without Buildings - Any Other City

Tugboat Records, 2001, indie rock

26th February 2021


Today is the 20th anniversary of Any Other City, Life Without Buildings' sole album and my most played record of 2021. Sonically, the band play a post-John Cale Velvets style of indie rock with influence from math rock and post-punk. While that may not sound very unique, what sets this Glasgow band apart from other turn-of-the-century indie bands is frontwoman Sue Tompkins. Her idiosyncratic style sees no need for verse-chorus form, opting instead for a stream of consciousness that strings words together, repeats phrases endlessly and enunciates varyingly. It's erratic but expressive as she delivers a collage of lovesick lyrics your English professor would love to pore over. From my experience, Any Other City is a rather polarising affair with friends of mine finding it annoying for the same reasons I find it charming. 'The Leanover' is an ideal starting point with the group's signature style on full display to hypnotic effect.
