The Avalanches - We Will Always Love You

Modular Recordings, 2020, neo-psychedelia/electronic

3rd January 2021


Australian band The Avalanches returned just in time for their country's summer and although it's not sunny at all here in the UK, I've had We Will Always Love You on repeat for the past month. The new record is less of a plunderphonics effort as the band cited the time and cost of sample clearances, instead opting for a Gorillaz-like feature list with guests ranging from Vashti Bunyan to Denzel Curry. Like their debut's flight-hopping hunt for love and Wildflower's road trip, this album is yet another journey, but this time into the depths of space, inspired by the Voyager Golden Record project. It's a reflective, emotive record with both blissful and melancholic moments, achieved through impressive features and a mix of electronic sounds tinged with psychedelia. 'The Divine Chord' is my favourite with MGMT and Johnny Marr aiding the group for some infectious psychedelic pop. If you're looking for something to lift your spirits, We Will Always Love You might just do the trick with its much needed celebration of life.
