Tiger Trap - Tiger Trap

K Records, 1993, twee pop

13th January 2021


Twee pop was a delightful moment in indie music and I'd forgotten how much I loved it. I've been revisiting those old releases from K Records and one particular album stands above the rest. Named after a Beat Happening song, Tiger Trap was formed in 1992, released their sole self-titled album in 1993 and quickly disbanded. Saccharine vocals combine with sweet but spiky guitar lines to make for a sugar rush of a record. I sometimes forget that music doesn't have to be all heavy and serious and indeed, this isn't. Instead, it's romantic, playful and innocent. It's the soundtrack to being hopelessly in love. Packed with earworms like 'Puzzle Pieces', Tiger Trap is a must-listen and my pick for the best twee pop record of all time.
